Principle of relevance is a concept in communication theory that  the communication process involved not only encoding, transfer, and decoding of messages but also numerous other elements including inference and context clues.

Infants are sensitive to caregiver responsiveness but also disruptions to the flow of natural interactions. Children with higher interruptions in face-to-face interactions may experience language delays.

experiment design - play two scenes simultaneously for children as they hear an instruction to find a particular action. If new words are formed they should direct visual attention to the matching scene. Dependent measure is the report ion of time that children attend to the matching scene during trials.

Procedure 
Started with training the mothers on the pattern of answering phones. Also making sure the mothers were pronouncing each word and action correctly. For the absent of priori preference, children watched a short video while on mothers lap. For the teaching phase, mothers were given 60s to teach then transitioned to another after the phone call. The interrupted period was in middle of one of the 
teaching phases mothers received a phone call lasting 30s.
